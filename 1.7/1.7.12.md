(Near uniqueness). If $F,F':\bold R \to \bold R$ are monotone non-decreasing functions, show that $\mu _F=\mu _{F'}$ if and only if there exists a constant $\mathcal{C}\in \bold R$ such that $F_+(x)=F'_+(x)+\mathcal{C}$ and $F_-(x)=F'_-(x)+\mathcal{C}$ for all $x\in \bold R$. Note that this implies that the value of $F$ at its points of discontinuity are irrelevant for the purposes of determining the Lebesgue-Stieltjes measure $\mu _F$; in particular, $\mu _F=\mu _{F_+}=\mu _{F_-}$.
